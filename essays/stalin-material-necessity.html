By Irfan Khan

Abstract: This essay explores Stalin’s role as both a historical product and producer of material necessity in the dialectical flow of twentieth-century politics.

Introduction

Joseph Stalin remains one of the most polarizing figures of the twentieth century. His rise to power was not purely accidental nor purely ideological — it was the expression of material necessity under specific historical conditions.
